# Watermelon: Rind Pickles, Compressed Cubes, and Seeds

This is the zero-waste watermelon project for the menu: pickle the rind, compress the flesh so it eats extra juicy, and turn the seeds into a crunchy garnish. It can act as a cold side, snack, garnish kit, or bridge between the smoked fish salad and the richer mains.

## Core Idea

- Pickle the rind 2-4 days ahead.
- Vacuum-seal watermelon cubes with seasoned juice to compress them and make the texture denser and juicier.
- Toast the seeds and use them in either a savory garnish or a dessert crunch.

This is a classic Southern-style use for the rind, built around the idea that the usually discarded part of the watermelon can become a sharp, sweet-sour pickle. It is especially useful next to rich meats.

Unique/useful: Make this first. The rind improves after time in the brine and would be great with cola-braised short ribs, Buffalo mac, smoked fish Caesar, or a cheese/snack board.

## Working Recipe: Pickled Watermelon Rind

### Ingredients

- Rind from 1 medium watermelon
- 2 cups rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar
- 1 cup water
- 3/4 to 1 cup sugar
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt
- 1 thumb ginger, sliced
- 1 jalapeno or serrano, sliced
- 1 teaspoon coriander seed
- 1 teaspoon mustard seed
- 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
- 1 bay leaf
- Optional: strip of lime peel, star anise, or cinnamon stick

### Method

1. Peel away the dark green skin and leave the pale rind, with a little pink flesh attached if you like.
2. Cut into spears or small cubes.
3. Bring vinegar, water, sugar, salt, ginger, chile, and spices to a simmer.
4. Add rind and simmer gently until just translucent but still crisp, about 5-10 minutes depending on thickness.
5. Transfer rind and brine to jars or containers, cool, and refrigerate.
6. Let sit at least overnight. Better after 2-3 days.

### Flavor Directions

- Southern-ish: apple cider vinegar, sugar, mustard seed, celery seed, chile.
- Bright/Asian-ish: rice vinegar, ginger, chile, coriander, lime peel.
- Dessert-adjacent: cinnamon, star anise, clove, lemon peel, less chile.

## Working Recipe: Compressed Watermelon Cubes

### Ingredients

- Watermelon flesh, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 to 1 1/2 cups watermelon juice
- 1-2 tablespoons lime juice
- 1-2 teaspoons sugar or honey, optional
- Pinch of salt
- Optional savory direction: splash of rice vinegar, a few drops sesame oil, chile crisp oil, mint stems
- Optional dessert direction: basil, lime zest, vanilla, or a tiny pinch of black pepper

### Method

1. Blend and strain some watermelon to make juice.
2. Season the juice with lime and salt. Keep it just slightly more intense than you want the final fruit to taste.
3. Put watermelon cubes in a vacuum bag in a single layer.
4. Add enough seasoned juice to coat and surround the cubes without making the bag impossible to seal.
5. Vacuum seal. For maximum compression, chill for at least 2 hours and up to 24 hours.
6. Drain before serving, reserving the liquid for agua fresca, cocktails, or a quick dressing.

### Serving Ideas

- Toss with mint, scallions, sesame, lime, and chili crisp.
- Serve with pickled rind and toasted seeds as a watermelon "poke."
- Spoon beside smoked fish Caesar as a cold, juicy counterpoint.
- Use as a dessert garnish with panna cotta if the seasoning is kept sweet/herbal.

## Working Recipe: Toasted Watermelon Seeds

### Basic Method

1. Rinse seeds well and remove clinging fruit.
2. Dry thoroughly on a towel or sheet pan. Overnight drying is ideal.
3. Optional: simmer 5-10 minutes in salted water, then drain and dry again.
4. Toast in a dry skillet over medium heat or on a sheet pan at 325-350°F until nutty and crisp.
5. Toss while warm with a little oil or butter and seasoning.

## Seed Uses

### Savory: Caesar Crunch

Toss toasted seeds with olive oil, Old Bay, lemon zest, garlic powder, and a pinch of sugar. Use with rye breadcrumbs on the smoked whitefish Caesar.

### Savory: Watermelon Poke Crunch

Toss toasted seeds with sesame oil, soy powder or a few drops of soy sauce, nori flakes, chile flake, and lime zest. Sprinkle over compressed watermelon cubes with pickled rind.

### Dessert: Panna Cotta Crunch

Toss toasted seeds with butter, sugar, salt, and a tiny pinch of black pepper or cinnamon. Use sparingly over sweet corn panna cotta with blueberry sauce.

### Snack Bowl

Toss toasted seeds with butter, ranch powder, cayenne, and celery salt. Put them out near drinks.

## Menu Fit

Best version for this menu: make a composed watermelon side with compressed cubes, pickled rind, mint, scallions, lime, chili crisp, and sesame/nori watermelon seeds. It will be cold, crunchy, acidic, and weird in a useful way.

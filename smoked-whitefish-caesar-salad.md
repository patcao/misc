# Smoked Whitefish Caesar Salad

The exact phrase "smoked whitefish Caesar" is not common online, but there is a strong family of recipes that use smoked fish or smoked shellfish as the briny/smoky backbone of a Caesar-style salad. The useful move for this menu is to treat smoked whitefish like an anchovy-adjacent ingredient: salty, smoky, rich, and excellent with lemon, garlic, capers, Parmesan, and crisp lettuce.

## Recipe Leads

### Smoky Caesar Salad

- Source: Food & Wine
- Link: https://www.foodandwine.com/recipes/smoky-caesar-salad

This is the closest direct model. The dressing uses canned smoked trout where anchovies would normally sit, then builds the rest of the Caesar profile with Parmesan, egg yolk, lemon, capers, Dijon, Worcestershire, and garlic.

Unique/useful: It solves the apartment smoking problem by using already-smoked fish. Smoked whitefish could be swapped in, either blended into the dressing or flaked over the finished salad.

### Kohlrabi Caesar Salad

- Source: Bon Appetit
- Link: https://www.bonappetit.com/recipe/kohlrabi-caesar-salad

This uses smoked trout as the fish element and pairs it with lemon, aioli, herbs, Parmesan, and croutons. The crunchy kohlrabi makes it feel colder, brighter, and more picnic-friendly than a standard Caesar.

Unique/useful: The structure would translate well to romaine hearts with smoked whitefish, herbs, and a cold crunchy vegetable like celery, kohlrabi, fennel, or radish.

### Smoked Oyster Caesar

- Source: Epicurious
- Link: https://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/smoked-oyster-caesar-salad

This recipe replaces anchovies with canned smoked oysters and uses the reserved smoky oil to toast oyster crackers. It keeps the core Caesar logic but pushes the smoky seafood note harder.

Unique/useful: The technique is the prize: use the smoke-rich oil or fat from the seafood to season the crunch. For this menu, that points toward Old Bay oyster crackers, rye breadcrumbs, or potato-chip croutons.

### Smoked Trout Salad

- Source: Bon Appetit / Wise Sons Jewish Delicatessen
- Link: https://www.bonappetit.com/recipe/smoked-trout-salad

This is a deli-style smoked fish salad with onion, celery, mayo, sour cream, herbs, and lemon. The recipe notes that whitefish can stand in for trout.

Unique/useful: This is a bridge between whitefish salad and Caesar. A version could sit on romaine wedges, or be loosened with lemon, Worcestershire, Parmesan, and olive oil into a Caesar-ish dressing.

### Baja-Style Caesar Salad

- Source: Food & Wine / Claudette Zepeda
- Link: https://www.foodandwine.com/baja-style-caesar-salad-6421022

This leans into Caesar salad's Tijuana roots with lime, Cotija, anchovies, romaine hearts, and chicharrones. It is bright, salty, crunchy, and casual.

Unique/useful: The lime-Cotija-chicharron direction could make the smoked whitefish version feel more July 4 and snacky. Crushed tortilla chips or salt-and-vinegar chips would also work.

### Smoked Trout and Crispy Potato Cakes with Capers, Caraway and Dill

- Source: The Guardian / Rukmini Iyer
- Link: https://www.theguardian.com/food/2025/jun/02/rukmini-iyer-quick-easy-recipe-smoked-trout-crisp-potato-cakes-capers-caraway-dill

This is not a Caesar, but the flavor map is relevant: smoked fish, capers, dill, lemon, creme fraiche, and crisp potato. It points toward a more deli/picnic interpretation of the salad.

Unique/useful: Crispy potato cakes or hash-brown shards could replace croutons and make the salad feel like whitefish salad plus Caesar plus potato salad.

## Menu Notes

- Best direction: romaine hearts, smoked whitefish, sharp Caesar dressing, rye breadcrumbs, fried capers, shaved celery, and lemon.
- Apartment smoke idea: smoke the mayo, Parmesan, rye crumbs, or salt rather than trying to cold-smoke fish safely at home.
- Optional crunch: everything-spiced or Old Bay toasted watermelon seeds.
